# Your Website Operations Manual 🚀

Welcome to your new digital storefront! This guide is written specifically for you (no coding experience required). It explains how to log in, manage your content, and keep your website running smoothly without needing to call a developer every time.

## 1. Logging Into Your Dashboard

Your website's content is powered by a system called **Sanity**. This is your control room for adding products, updating text, or writing blog posts.

1. Go to your custom Studio URL (usually `studio.yourdomain.com` or `yourdomain.com/studio`).
2. Log in using the email address you provided to our team, or via Google/GitHub authentication.
3. Once inside, you'll see a clean dashboard specifically tailored to your brand's data.

## 2. Managing Your Content

Think of your dashboard like a digital filing cabinet. 

*   **Pages & Formatting:** Use the left sidebar to navigate between different sections (e.g., "Blog Posts," "Testimonials," "Team Members").
*   **The Editor:** When you click on an entry, it opens an editor on the right. Simply type your changes as you would in Microsoft Word or Google Docs.
*   **Drafts vs. Published:** Every change you make is auto-saved as a *Draft*. Your live website will **not** change until you explicitly click the green **Publish** button at the bottom right.

## 3. Image Optimization Best Practices

Large images can slow down your site and hurt your Google ranking. Always follow these simple rules before uploading a photo:

1. **Size Matters:** Never upload a photo larger than 2MB or wider than 2500 pixels.
2. **Compression:** Run your lifestyle photos through a free tool like [TinyPNG](https://tinypng.com) or [Squoosh](https://squoosh.app) to shrink the file size before uploading it to Sanity. 
3. **Format:** Stick to JPG for regular photos, and PNG *only* if the image needs a transparent background (like a logo). Our system automatically converts your uploads to lightning-fast modern formats behind the scenes.
4. **Always Use Alt-Text:** The system will prompt you for an "Alt Text" description when uploading an image. Always write a brief, 1-sentence description of what the image shows (e.g., "A bottle of Vitamin C gummies on a marble counter"). This is vital for visually impaired users and SEO.

## 4. Understanding the Update Cycle

Because your site is "statically generated" (meaning it is pre-built for maximum lightning speeds), changes might not appear on your live URL the exact second you hit Publish.
*   **The Build Process:** When you hit Publish, it silently tells our servers to rebuild the website with your new content.
*   **Wait Time:** It usually takes about 1-2 minutes for the new version of your site to go live globally. Please be patient!

## 5. Support & Troubleshooting

If you ever feel lost or accidentally delete something:
1. Check the **History** tab in the top-right corner of the Sanity editor to revert to an older version seamlessly.
2. If the site is throwing an error, do not panic. The site will stay online using its last healthy backup while you fix the typo in the dashboard.
3. Still stuck? Reach out to your dedicated account manager for a hands-on walkthrough!
